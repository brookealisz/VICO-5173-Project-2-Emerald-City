Pike Place Market

85 Pike St, Seattle, WA 98101
Pike Place Market is one of the most iconic spots in all of the Pacific Northwest. Take a stroll through downtown toward the waterfront of the Puget Sound to indulge in the many vendors the historic neighborhood has to offer. Perhaps stop into the first-ever Starbucks, buy some fresh produce, or shop at a novelty store you never knew you needed.

CASCADE MOUNTAINS

Snoqualmie Region — North Bend Area
The Cascade Mountains are one of two major mountain ranges in the Pacific Northwest. Within them lie Mount Rainier, the regions tallest and most famous peak, sitting high above the clouds at 14,411 feet. Two national parks rest in the range, the North Cascades National Park and Mount Rainier National Park, where a variety of trails are available to be hiked from the Spring until the Fall.

STARBUCKS RESERVE ROASTERY

1124 Pike St, Seattle, WA 98101
Nestled in the bustling neighborhood of Capitol Hill, the worlds first Starbucks Reserve Roastery boasts a full cafe, bar, gift shop, experimental drink area, seating galore, and roasting lab. Order a coffee and watch the beans roast or have a relaxing brunch in the coffee-shop aesthetic you’ve always been dreaming of. Plus— the Roastery serves exclusive drinks and flavors that aren’t available at regular Starbucks locations!

AMAZON SPHERES

2111 7th Ave, Seattle, WA 98121
Designed and created from 2013-2018, the Amazon Spheres of Seattle house over 26,000 plants in this experimental and innovative space. Amazon employees (and once a month, the general public) are welcome and encouraged to work within the biophilic walls. The Spheres require constant care and attention by a skilled team of horticulturists to maintain the plant life. The space is equipped with chairs, tables, and even a cafe to curate the most unique work environment the city has ever seen.

GAS WORKS PARK

2101 N Northlake Way, Seattle, WA 98103
The eerie yet beautiful structures of a coal-gas plant that ceased operations in 1956 loom over Lake Union, the remains of which have been turned into a public park with some of the best views that Seattle has to offer. The park includes a deck over the water, bike paths, and Kite Hill, an impressive mound embedded with a large metal compass on its peak. The site is known as a historical landmark and is one of the first postindustrial landscapes to be transformed into a public park.

SPACE NEEDLE

400 Broad St, Seattle, WA 98109
Perhaps the most iconic building in all of the Pacific Northwest, the Space Needle rises 605 feet into the air and offers a 360º view of the city. Walk on the glass floor of the viewing deck or experience the worlds only revolving glass floor in the cocktail lounge— wherever you choose to step, Seattle and the skies are with you.
